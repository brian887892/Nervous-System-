🧠 central nervous system · 100 Q&A

based exclusively on lecture notes — 50 MCQs + 50 structured (with answers)
1 According to the text, the nervous system is anatomically and functionally divided into which two main parts?
  • a) Somatic and Autonomic
  • b) Brain and Spinal Cord
  • c) Central and Peripheral
  • d) Sensory and Motor
✓ answer: c) Central and Peripheral
2 What is the largest and most developed area of the human brain?
  • a) Cerebrum
  • b) Cerebellum
  • c) Diencephalon
  • d) Brainstem
✓ answer: a) Cerebrum
3 Which lobe of the cerebrum is primarily concerned with the interpretation and integration of body sensory inputs?
  • a) Frontal Lobe
  • b) Occipital Lobe
  • c) Temporal Lobe
  • d) Parietal Lobe
✓ answer: d) Parietal Lobe
4 The primary visual cortex for visual information interpretation is located in which lobe?
  • a) Frontal Lobe
  • b) Occipital Lobe
  • c) Parietal Lobe
  • d) Insula Lobe
✓ answer: b) Occipital Lobe
5 The grooves on the surface of the cerebrum are called:
  • a) Gyri
  • b) Fissures
  • c) Sulci
  • d) Tracts
✓ answer: c) Sulci
6 Which gyrus on the frontal lobe is known as the Primary Motor Cortex?
  • a) Postcentral gyrus
  • b) Cingulate gyrus
  • c) Precentral gyrus
  • d) Hippocampus
✓ answer: c) Precentral gyrus
7 In most people, which hemisphere of the cerebrum is dominant for language and tasks involving symbolic reasoning?
  • a) The Left Hemisphere
  • b) The Right Hemisphere
  • c) Both equally
  • d) It varies randomly
✓ answer: a) The Left Hemisphere
8 The corpus callosum is the largest set of which type of fibers?
  • a) Association Fibers
  • b) Projection Fibers
  • c) Commissural Fibers
  • d) Arcuate Fibers
✓ answer: c) Commissural Fibers
9 The Reticular Activating System (RAS), which maintains consciousness and alertness, is predominantly located in the Pons and which other structure?
  • a) Cerebellum
  • b) Thalamus
  • c) Medulla Oblongata
  • d) Midbrain
✓ answer: c) Medulla Oblongata
10 Which part of the brain is described as the "emotional brain"?
  • a) The Reticular System
  • b) The Limbic System
  • c) The Corpus Callosum
  • d) The Pineal Gland
✓ answer: b) The Limbic System
11 The hippocampus, important for learning and memory, is located deep in which lobe?
  • a) Frontal lobe
  • b) Parietal lobe
  • c) Occipital lobe
  • d) Temporal lobe
✓ answer: d) Temporal lobe
12 According to the text, high, prolonged levels of which hormone are associated with shrinkage of the hippocampus?
  • a) Melatonin
  • b) Dopamine
  • c) Cortisol
  • d) Oxytocin
✓ answer: c) Cortisol
13 The almond-shaped nuclei with a primary role in detecting threatening stimuli is the:
  • a) Hippocampus
  • b) Thalamus
  • c) Amygdala
  • d) Cingulate Gyrus
✓ answer: c) Amygdala
14 Which part of the diencephalon acts as the "gateway" to the cerebral cortex?
  • a) Hypothalamus
  • b) Thalamus
  • c) Epithalamus
  • d) Pineal Gland
✓ answer: b) Thalamus
15 The body's thermostat and set point for temperature regulation is located in the:
  • a) Thalamus
  • b) Pons
  • c) Cerebellum
  • d) Hypothalamus
✓ answer: d) Hypothalamus
16 The text notes that the Blood Brain Barrier is missing in one region to allow it to assess blood conditions directly. This region is the:
  • a) Medulla Oblongata
  • b) Cerebellum
  • c) Hypothalamus
  • d) Midbrain
✓ answer: c) Hypothalamus
17 Which hormone, secreted by the pineal gland, is suppressed by blue light from screens?
  • a) Cortisol
  • b) Dopamine
  • c) Melatonin
  • d) ADH
✓ answer: c) Melatonin
18 Damage to the substantia nigra of the midbrain has been linked to which disease?
  • a) Alzheimer's disease
  • b) Parkinson's disease
  • c) Huntington's disease
  • d) Multiple sclerosis
✓ answer: b) Parkinson's disease
19 The corpora quadrigemina, involved in visual and auditory reflexes, is located in the:
  • a) Cerebellum
  • b) Pons
  • c) Medulla Oblongata
  • d) Midbrain
✓ answer: d) Midbrain
20 The superior colliculi are involved in processing which type of information?
  • a) Auditory
  • b) Olfactory
  • c) Visual
  • d) Gustatory
✓ answer: c) Visual
21 The cerebellum accounts for about 10% of the brain's volume, yet contains over what percentage of the total number of neurons?
  • a) 25%
  • b) 50%
  • c) 75%
  • d) 90%
✓ answer: b) 50%
22 The inner, branching white matter of the cerebellum is called the:
  • a) Cerebral peduncles
  • b) Arbor vitae
  • c) Cerebellar peduncles
  • d) Corpora quadrigemina
✓ answer: b) Arbor vitae
23 Which structure receives information from proprioceptors to help maintain balance and posture?
  • a) Cerebrum
  • b) Thalamus
  • c) Cerebellum
  • d) Hypothalamus
✓ answer: c) Cerebellum
24 According to the text, which part of the brain is responsible for refining a practiced activity into a learned skilled routine?
  • a) Cerebrum
  • b) Midbrain
  • c) Cerebellum
  • d) Medulla Oblongata
✓ answer: c) Cerebellum
25 The pons contains two pontine respiratory centers that help fine-tune breathing. What are they?
  • a) Inspiratory and Expiratory centers
  • b) Pneumotaxic and Apneustic centers
  • c) Cardiac and Vasomotor centers
  • d) Dorsal and Ventral centers
✓ answer: b) Pneumotaxic and Apneustic centers
26 Which cranial nerve is the largest of the 12 pairs and is functionally associated with the pons?
  • a) Vagus nerve (X)
  • b) Facial nerve (VII)
  • c) Trigeminal nerve (V)
  • d) Vestibulocochlear nerve (VIII)
✓ answer: c) Trigeminal nerve (V)
27 The vital centers for cardiac, vasomotor, and respiratory regulation are located in which part of the brain?
  • a) Pons
  • b) Midbrain
  • c) Cerebellum
  • d) Medulla Oblongata
✓ answer: d) Medulla Oblongata
28 The phrenic nerve, which controls the diaphragm for inspiration, is formed from fibers of which plexus?
  • a) Brachial plexus
  • b) Lumbar plexus
  • c) Cervical plexus
  • d) Sacral plexus
✓ answer: c) Cervical plexus
29 What is the largest nerve in the body, arising from the sacral plexus?
  • a) Femoral nerve
  • b) Radial nerve
  • c) Sciatic nerve
  • d) Vagus nerve
✓ answer: c) Sciatic nerve
30 The ulnar and median nerves are branches of which plexus?
  • a) Cervical plexus
  • b) Brachial plexus
  • c) Lumbar plexus
  • d) Sacral plexus
✓ answer: b) Brachial plexus
31 Cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) is a filtrate of what?
  • a) Lymph
  • b) Blood
  • c) Interstitial fluid
  • d) Cytoplasm
✓ answer: b) Blood
32 Which of the following is NOT a stated role of CSF in the CNS?
  • a) Cushioning delicate nervous tissue
  • b) Providing buoyancy to the brain
  • c) Producing neurotransmitters for synaptic transmission
  • d) Exchanging gases, nutrients, and wastes
✓ answer: c) Producing neurotransmitters for synaptic transmission
33 CSF is primarily created by the choroid plexuses located in the:
  • a) Subarachnoid space
  • b) Dural venous sinuses
  • c) Ventricles of the brain
  • d) Central canal of the spinal cord
✓ answer: c) Ventricles of the brain
34 How many ventricles are there in the brain?
  • a) Two
  • b) Three
  • c) Four
  • d) Five
✓ answer: c) Four
35 What is the average volume of CSF in circulation in an adult at any one time?
  • a) 50 ml
  • b) 150 ml
  • c) 250 ml
  • d) 500 ml
✓ answer: b) 150 ml
36 Approximately how much CSF is made per day in an adult?
  • a) 150 ml
  • b) 250 ml
  • c) 500 ml
  • d) 1000 ml
✓ answer: c) 500 ml
37 From the lateral ventricles, CSF flows through the interventricular foramen to reach which structure?
  • a) Cerebral aqueduct
  • b) Fourth ventricle
  • c) Third ventricle
  • d) Subarachnoid space
✓ answer: c) Third ventricle
